Knowledge Archive

Aryabhata was born in 476 CE and became one of the most influential scholars of ancient India. He studied mathematics and astronomy and later composed the Aryabhatiya.

Introduced methods for solving equations, advanced trigonometry, and produced one of the earliest accurate approximations of pi.

Explained eclipses scientifically and proposed that Earth rotates on its axis, a remarkable idea for the time.

His work influenced later Indian, Islamic, and global scientific developments for centuries.
