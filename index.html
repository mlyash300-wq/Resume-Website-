Aryabhata
Mathematician • Astronomer • Scientific Pioneer

Born: 476 CE | Ancient India | Age of Gupta Empire

Professional Summary
Aryabhata was a pioneering Indian mathematician and astronomer who introduced revolutionary concepts in algebra, trigonometry, and planetary motion. His discoveries formed the backbone of ancient and modern scientific computation systems.
Detailed Profile
Aryabhata worked at Kusumapura (modern Patna). He wrote the Aryabhatiya at age 23. His model proposed Earth rotates on its axis — centuries ahead of Europe.
He introduced methods for solving quadratic equations, approximating π, and calculating sine tables using systematic increments.
His astronomical system explained eclipses as shadows rather than mythical demons, marking a scientific revolution.
